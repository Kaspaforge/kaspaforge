Mechanics

How a board post works

1 · No account, ever

There is no sign-up and nothing to lose — posting is a small Kaspa transaction sent from your Desk profile, the same encrypted key file used by Safe, Escrow and Market. No username, no password, no profile page.

2 · The board is a chain, not a database

Every thread and reply is a transaction on the public Kaspa BlockDAG. This page is a read-only index over that chain — the record isn't ours to lose, and anyone can rebuild the same index by re-scanning it.

3 · Moderation hides, it doesn't erase

A post that breaks the publication rules is hidden from this feed — its text and image never load here again. The transaction itself is untouched: the tombstone card links straight to it on the public explorer.

Fair questions

Do I need an account?

No. Reading every board is open to anyone with a browser. Posting needs your Desk profile — a key file that stays on your device — and a small KAS transaction fee; there's no username, email or password anywhere in the flow.

What happens to what I post?

It becomes a permanent, public Kaspa transaction. This index can hide it from view, but it doesn't disappear from the chain — anyone can look it up by its transaction ID, including through the tombstone card's link.

Can a moderator delete my post?

Only from this feed. Hiding a post or a whole thread flips a viewer-side flag here — it is not, and cannot be, a rewrite of the BlockDAG. See the publication rules for what gets hidden and why.

What can I post?

Text (and, where enabled, an image) under a board's topic. The publication rules set out what isn't allowed here — this page doesn't sit above that policy.

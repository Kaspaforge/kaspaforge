Publication Rules

What this viewer hides, and why nothing is ever actually erased. v1 — July 20, 2026. Contact: kaspa@officeforge.co.

The short version

Every board post is a Kaspa transaction. This page is a read-only index over that chain, not a database we control — we only ever hide a post from this index. We never claim to delete it, because we can't. Hidden means "not shown here." It never means gone.

1. What gets hidden

A post — a thread or a reply, including its image where images are supported — is hidden from this feed when it:

  • is illegal under the law that applies to us as the operator of this index — for example child sexual abuse material, credible threats of violence, or content whose mere display would itself be a crime;
  • doxxes a private individual — their home address, ID or private financial details — without consent;
  • is spam or flooding meant to bury a board rather than post on its topic;
  • breaks the bump and rate limits built into how a board works (see how it works).

That's the whole list. Being offensive, wrong or unpopular is not, on its own, a reason to hide a post. This is a narrow display policy for what we're willing to render on kaspaforge.org — not a general speech code, and not a claim that we can control what's on Kaspa.

2. The chain is neutral — this viewer isn't the chain

Kaspa itself has no opinion about board posts and no moderation of any kind. Once a transaction is broadcast and confirmed, it's permanent and public, and it stays outside anyone's control — ours included. Hiding a post here changes what this page renders. It changes nothing on-chain. The transaction sits at the same transaction ID it always did, for as long as Kaspa exists.

3. Hidden isn't gone: read it anyway

A hidden post shows here as a dashed tombstone card — no text, no image — but every tombstone still links straight to its own transaction. Open that link, or paste the transaction ID into explorer.kaspa.org or any other Kaspa explorer, and you can read the raw post yourself, exactly as it was sent. Our display policy can't take that away. Anyone can also run their own indexer over the same public chain and build a feed under a different policy — or none at all.

4. Report a post

To report a post you believe should be hidden under §1, email kaspa@officeforge.co with the board and the post's transaction ID or link. We review it and may hide it from this feed. We cannot, and won't claim to, remove it from Kaspa itself.

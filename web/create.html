Delay & inheritance

The delay is the window during which a theft can be cancelled. Longer is safer, but you wait longer for your own withdrawals too.

Three keys — two in your key file, one on a card

🔑 No recovery sheet to save. The hot and funding keys and this vault are saved automatically to your encrypted master key file — your profile in the Desk. Back it up once from the Desk ("Export key file"). The alarm key is the deliberate exception: it lives on a separate alarm card below, apart from the file — so that stealing your key file and password is never enough to empty the vault.

Hot key — starts withdrawals and check-ins
Fine to keep on your computer or in a password manager.
Alarm key — cancels a theft · your alarm card
Shown once — NOT saved to your key file, by design. Hot + alarm together move the vault instantly, so whoever holds your key file must not automatically hold both. Save this card on paper or a separate drive — apart from the key file and its password.
Keep it SEPARATE from the hot key: paper, a safe, another device. If a thief gets both, the protection is gone.
Funding key — temporary, to place coins into the vault
Used once on step 3. Not important afterwards.

Place the coins

Send KAS from any wallet to the funding address. Once it arrives, hit "Lock into vault" — your browser assembles and signs the on-chain contract.

Whose address is this? Yours. Its funding key was just generated in this browser and is saved in your master key file — we have no access to these coins. They simply wait here until you press "Lock into vault": at that moment your browser itself assembles and signs the vault contract. A covenant can't be created by a plain transfer — that's why this step exists (and why you should never send KAS directly to the vault address).

🔑 The private key to this address is yours — it's saved in your master key file (your Desk profile).

If something goes wrong

A claim is a process, not a grab.

The holder describes the damage or breach and supports the requested amount with evidence. Filing a claim freezes the automatic return while the case is resolved.

1
Claim filedThe holder states what happened and how much they request.
2
Evidence revealedA party deliberately shares the relevant encrypted chat and media.
3
AI proposes an outcomeFull return, holder payout or a proportional split.
4
Agreement or human rulingA party signs an accepted proposal; otherwise a human arbiter decides.
The authority boundary

AI advises. It never moves money.

The mediator has no wallet or signing keys. Its proposal is non-binding. A party must accept and sign, or the case goes to a human arbiter.

If no arbiter ruling arrives by the contract deadline, the emergency timeout returns everything to the depositor without a service fee.

FAQ

The questions that matter before you start

Who holds the deposit?

Neither side and not Kaspa Forge. The KAS is locked in an on-chain contract until an allowed return, settlement, claim verdict or timeout path is used.

What happens if nobody does anything?

After the term and claim window pass with no claim, the deposit returns to the depositor automatically. The holder does not need to approve it.

When can the holder file a claim?

At any point during the term and during the final claim window of 3, 7, 14 or 30 days. The holder should document the breach and the amount requested.

Can the holder simply keep it?

No. Keeping any part requires the depositor to accept and sign a settlement or a human arbiter to rule on a filed claim. A split is possible when only part of the claim is proven.

What if Kaspa Forge or the arbiter disappears?

The contract has an emergency deadline. If a filed claim receives no ruling by then, the deposit returns to the depositor automatically without a service fee.

What do I need to keep safe?

Your encrypted Desk key file and its password. The deal key is stored in that profile. Kaspa Forge never stores or asks for your private signing keys.

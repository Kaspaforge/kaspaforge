🚨 A withdrawal has started

Cancel it with your alarm key

Got a "withdrawal started" alert and it wasn't you? You have the entire cancel window from the moment it started (you chose its length at creation; the timer is visible in "My vault"). And you don't need your own device: any computer or a borrowed phone works — your alarm card is enough.

  1. Don't panic. The coins can't move until the cancel window ends.
  2. Open My vault — from any device (check the address bar first — see below).
  3. Find the active withdrawal and how much time is left.
  4. Press ⛔ Cancel withdrawal and enter the alarm key from your alarm card — the "Alarm key" line on the card you saved at creation (it is deliberately not stored in the key file). Older vaults that kept the alarm key in the profile: it's already inside — you only confirm with your password, or import your master key file on another device (Desk → Import).
  5. Never enter keys or your Desk password on any site other than kaspaforge.org.
  6. After the cancel — don't relax. If you didn't start that withdrawal, the thief has your hot key and will try again. Create a new vault right away and withdraw everything into it with a normal withdrawal: the thief can't cancel your withdrawal — they don't have the alarm key. Keep the alarm card at hand until the window closes.
  7. Faster escape (advanced): since you hold both keys — the hot key in your key file and the alarm key on your card — the covenant's migrate path can move the whole vault to a fresh one instantly, without waiting out any window. It's available in the open-source CLI: vaultctl migrate (block below). The thief can't use it: migrate demands both signatures, and the alarm key exists only on your card.

Recover from your master key file (the simple way)

Your vaults, deals and listings all live in one encrypted master key file — your Desk profile. On any device, open your Desk, choose Import / restore and load your master key file (.age) with its password — everything comes back at once. Back it up from the Desk ("Export key file"); that one file is your recovery. Even things you created after that export come back: keys are derived from the master seed inside the file, and the Desk automatically re-discovers your vaults, deals and listings from the server on import.

The one deliberate exception: vault alarm keys. They live on the alarm cards you saved at creation — not in the file and not derivable from it (so stealing the file and password is never enough to empty a vault). The Desk marks such vaults "alarm: card"; cancelling a theft or migrating asks for the key from the card.

Have an older .txt recovery sheet from before this change, or just one vault to reopen? The manual steps below still work.

Need the raw keys out of the file — even if this site is gone? Download the offline key-file decryptor (a single HTML file, ~3 MB): open it from disk with your internet off, load the .age file and its password — it shows every private key inside (except alarm keys of card vaults — those exist only on their cards) without any network requests. The file is standard age, so the CLI works too: age -d keyfile.age. Also mirrored in the open-source repo: github.com/pcdoctormsk-ctrl/kaspa-safe.

Reopen a single vault from an old recovery sheet (legacy)

For vaults created in the era of paper .txt recovery sheets, before the master key file. Your vault isn't stored in a browser — it lives on the blockchain, so it reopens from the sheet's public values. Just to look, no private keys are needed. (For key-file vaults you don't need this: import the file on the Desk.)

What you need (all printed on the recovery sheet)
  • public hot key — the "Hot pubkey" line
  • public alarm key — the "Alarm pubkey" line
  • withdrawal delay — the number from the "Withdrawal delay" line (DAA are Kaspa's time "ticks", ~10 per second; enter the number as printed)
  • if inheritance was on: the heir's pubkey, the period (DAA number) and the mode — the "mode: automatic / manual" part
  • newer sheets also print a "Fee budget" line — it is part of the address; sheets without it mean the default (1,000,000). If your sheet has a different value, load the sheet as a file ("Load recovery sheet") instead of typing — it applies everything automatically
  • private keys — only when you actually act (withdraw / cancel / check in)
Steps
  1. Open My vault.
  2. Enter the hot pubkey and the alarm pubkey.
  3. Enter the delay (DAA); if inheritance was on — the heir's pubkey and the period.
  4. Set the "Automatic inheritance" checkbox exactly as the sheet says: mode "automatic" — checked, "manual" — unchecked.
  5. Press Open — the panel recomputes the vault address.
  6. Compare the address with the sheet and check the balance. Match? You're in.
Address doesn't match the sheet, or balance shows 0? Almost always it's the inheritance-mode checkbox or a typo in the period. Nothing is broken or lost: fix it and press "Open" again. Still off with inheritance disabled? Type the sheet into a plain .txt file and use "Load recovery sheet" — it matches the printed address automatically.

I'm the heir

Someone left you a Kaspa Safe vault. The owner set a period: as long as they kept checking in ("I'm alive"), the vault was theirs alone. If the check-ins stopped — after the period, the vault opens to you. Before that you can't see or take anything; that's how the contract is designed.

"Automatic" modeNothing to do. After the period, the coins arrive at your Kaspa address on their own — the one the owner entered at creation. Just watch your wallet.
"Manual" modeOpen the vault in "My vault" — from the owner's master key file (import it on the Desk) or from an old recovery sheet (block above). Once the period expires, an "Inheritance" block appears: enter the private key of your own address (export it from your wallet — Kaspium and kaspa-ng can) and press "Claim the inheritance". The funds go strictly to your address — the contract allows nothing else.

How long is left — visible in the panel (the "Timer" row) or via vaultctl status. No permission from us is needed: the blockchain enforces the rules. Site down — vaultctl inherit does the same (block below).

If the site is unavailable — offline recovery with vaultctl

Kaspa Safe doesn't depend on us: the vault is an on-chain contract, and it checks only your keys and time. The open-source vaultctl tool does everything the site does, from a terminal, against any Kaspa v2+ node (running with --utxoindex). The code is published: github.com/pcdoctormsk-ctrl/kaspa-safe — keep a copy (Code → Download ZIP) and you won't depend even on GitHub.

# once: build it (needs Rust from rustup.rs, protobuf-compiler, clang)
git clone https://github.com/pcdoctormsk-ctrl/kaspa-safe
cd kaspa-safe/vaultctl && cargo build --release

# cargo build also produces kaspa-unlock. Have only the master key file (.age)?
# decrypt it once, then pull out the vault you need — vaultctl reads JSON too:
kaspa-unlock decrypt kaspa-office-profile.age profile.json   # asks your Desk password
jq '.vaults[0]' profile.json > vault.json                 # pick a vault; use vault.json below in place of sheet.txt

# the recovery input is fed AS IS — an old .txt sheet OR the vault.json above
# vault state: balance, age, inheritance timer
vaultctl status   --recovery kaspa-safe-recovery-XXXX.txt

# start a withdrawal to your address (needs the hot key — it's on the sheet)
vaultctl initiate --recovery sheet.txt --to kaspa:q...

# cancel an in-flight withdrawal (alarm key; --dest = the address the
# withdrawal is going TO — yours you know, a thief's is in the TG alert / panel)
vaultctl cancel   --recovery sheet.txt --dest kaspa:q...

# deliver a matured withdrawal to its destination (no key needed)
vaultctl complete --recovery sheet.txt --dest kaspa:q...

# "I'm alive" check-in · deliver the inheritance after the period
vaultctl checkin  --recovery sheet.txt
vaultctl inherit  --recovery sheet.txt              # "automatic": no key
vaultctl inherit  --recovery sheet.txt --heir-sk KEY # "manual": heir's key

# migrate — BOTH keys (hot + alarm) = instant full authority: move the whole vault
# anywhere with no delay (rotate a leaked key, upgrade the contract version, escape).
# --dest rescues a mid-withdrawal UTXO from the unvault address.
vaultctl migrate  --recovery vault.json --to kaspa:q... [--dest kaspa:q...]

Card vaults: vault.json extracted from the profile deliberately has no alarm key — before cancel or migrate, add it from your alarm card as an "alarm_sk" field (or feed an old-style .txt sheet, which carries it).

Useful flags: --node grpc://your-node:16110 — any Kaspa v2+ node with --utxoindex (defaults to the public OfficeForge node grpc://node.kaspaforge.org:16110 — or run your own); --dry-run — build and sign the transaction, print its txid, but do NOT broadcast (try this first). To verify the contract itself: vaultctl selftest — 25 checks of every path and attack inside the node VM.

If a key is lost

Hot key lostThe coins are not stolen, but you can't start a normal withdrawal anymore. If inheritance is on — simply stop checking in: after the period the coins go to the heir automatically ("automatic" mode) or the heir claims them with their key ("manual"). The heir can be you — if you named your own second address. If inheritance is off — honestly: the coins are frozen forever, nobody (including us) can reach them. That's why we recommend enabling inheritance even "to yourself". Keep your master key file and its Desk password backed up.
Alarm card lostYour money is safe: withdrawing with the hot key and inheritance still work. What's gone is the ability to cancel a theft and the instant migrate path. Don't wait: move the KAS with a normal withdrawal (sit out the window) into a new vault with a fresh alarm card.
Both keys lostAccess is gone forever (unless inheritance is on — see "hot key lost"). OfficeForge cannot recover keys — nobody can. That's why you keep your master key file (and its password) backed up.

What we will never ask for

If anyone asks you to send a private key, a seed phrase, your Desk password, your master key file or your alarm card — it's a scammer.

The alarm card deserves special care: together with the key file it gives instant full control of the vault (the migrate path). Nobody legitimate will ever ask to "check" or "verify" it.

Your master key file does not give OfficeForge access to your coins — it's encrypted with your password and exists for you alone, to sign actions with your own keys. We don't store private keys or your password and can't recover them for you. And we will never ask for them — not in chat, not by email, not "for verification". Support (kaspa@officeforge.co) can explain the steps, but physically has no access to keys or coins.
